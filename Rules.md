## 📋 Waste Categories & Preparation Guidelines

### 1. Paper
**Examples**: Newspapers, office paper, magazines, notebooks  
**Symbols**: None (natural fiber)  
**Key Notes**: 
- Keep paper clean and dry. Avoid mixing with food or liquid to maintain recycling quality.
- Remove plastic windows from envelopes
- Shred sensitive documents before recycling
- Pizza boxes: clean cardboard parts only (greasy sections → compost)

### 2. Cardboard
**Examples**: Corrugated boxes, cereal cartons, shipping boxes, paperboard  
**Symbols**: None  
**Key Notes**:
- Remove or rinse off any food residue. Contamination can reduce recycling quality.
- Flatten boxes to save space in bins
- Remove tape and labels (if possible)
- Pizza boxes: tear off clean parts only

### 4. Glass
**Examples**: Bottles, jars (clear, green, brown), windows  
**Symbols**: None (color sorting)  
**Key Notes**:
- Sort by color (clear/green/brown) when required
- Infinitely recyclable without quality loss
- Remove metal lids/caps (recycle separately)
- Rinse clean, never break before disposal

### 5. Metal
**Examples**: Aluminum cans/foil, steel food cans, copper wire, aerosol cans  
**Symbols**: None or ♻️ Steel  
**Key Notes**:
- Metals are valuable and energy-efficient to recycle
- Magnets separate steel from aluminum (non-magnetic = aluminum)
- Rinse food cans, remove paper labels
- Crush aluminum cans to save space

```
[🥫 Photo Placeholder: Aluminum cans, steel cans, copper wire sorted]

### 6. Organic Waste
**Examples**: Food scraps, yard waste, wood scraps  
**Symbols**: Compost symbol 🌱  
**Key Notes**:
- Compost organic waste when possible (reduces landfill methane)
- No meat/dairy in home compost (attracts pests)
- Yard waste → green bin or compost facility
- Wood scraps → mulch or biomass energy

```

### 7. Electronics & Hazardous
**Examples**: Batteries, e-waste, bulbs, paint cans  
**Symbols**: ⚠️ Hazardous  
**Key Notes**:
- **Special disposal ONLY** - never in regular bins
- Batteries contain toxic chemicals (lithium, lead)
- E-waste has valuable rare earth metals
- Find local e-waste collection events

### 8. Textiles/Clothing
**Examples**: Old clothes, shoes, fabrics, curtains, bedsheets  
**Symbols**: None  
**Key Notes**:
- **Donate, reuse, or textile recycling bins** - never general waste
- Wash clean before donation
- Check for damage (wearable condition only)
- Shoes → specific donation bins
